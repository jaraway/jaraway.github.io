Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common questions regarding BMI and body metrics.

Is BMI accurate for gym-goers and athletes?

Not entirely. BMI looks purely at your weight and height. Because muscle tissue is significantly denser than fat tissue, highly active athletes with substantial muscle mass can sometimes register as "overweight" even with very low body fat percentages.

Does biological sex alter the core calculation?

No, the mathematical equation remains identical for adult men and women. However, women natively carry a slightly higher absolute body fat percentage required for standard endocrine and biological function compared to men at an identical numerical score.

What are the primary limitations of BMI?

BMI serves as a rapid general population health screen, but it fails to evaluate fat distribution (like dangerous visceral stomach fat vs subcutaneous tissue) or account for genetic differences in bone density or natural age-based metabolic shifts.
